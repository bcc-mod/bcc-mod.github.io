How-To

Required Tools

Soldering Iron

Hot Air Gun

Flux

Electronics Screwdriver Kit

Installation Process: Replacing the Default MCU (Video In-Progress)

  1. Disassemble the GameCube controller carefully using your electronics screwdriver kit.
  2. Locate the default MCU on the controller's circuit board.
  3. Apply flux around the MCU's pins to facilitate removal.
  4. Use the hot air gun to heat the MCU evenly, then carefully remove it from the board.
  5. Clean the board and prepare the pads for the new MCU.
  6. Position the BCC MCU onto the prepared pads.
  7. Use the soldering iron to carefully solder each pin of the new MCU to the board.
  8. Inspect your work to ensure all connections are secure and there are no shorts.
  9. Reassemble the controller and test all functions.
  10. Calibrate the new MCU using the BCC software for optimal performance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes BCC different from other GameCube controller mods?

BCC offers a comprehensive suite of performance enhancements, including joystick recalibration, snapback filtering, button remapping, and notch calibration, all in one package.

Is the BCC MCU compatible with all GameCube controllers?

The BCC MCU is designed to be compatible with original Nintendo GameCube controllers. Compatibility with third-party controllers may vary.

How difficult is it to install the BCC MCU?

Installation requires some technical skill and experience with soldering. We recommend having it installed by a professional if you're not comfortable with the process.

Does installing the BCC MCU void my controller's warranty?

Yes, modifying your controller by installing the BCC MCU will void the original manufacturer's warranty. However, we offer our own warranty on the BCC MCU itself.
